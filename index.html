About the Mission

Four astronauts will embark on Artemis II, NASA's inaugural crewed mission towards establishing a long-term lunar presence. This 10-day journey will serve as a pivotal test for NASA's deep space exploration capabilities, including the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft, now with astronauts on board.



Background

Artemis missions aim to achieve historic milestones: landing the first woman and first person of color on the Moon using cutting-edge technologies. This endeavor involves collaboration with both commercial and international partners to establish a sustained lunar presence. Furthermore, NASA's aspirations extend beyond the Moon, as they plan to utilize their newfound knowledge to propel humanity towards an even grander goal: sending astronauts to Mars. This return to the Moon is driven by a quest for scientific discoveries, economic advantages, and the profound inspiration it offers to a new generation of explorers known as the Artemis Generation. This global community is united in its dedication to exploring deep space for the betterment of all. The inaugural crewed Artemis mission, Artemis II, will witness astronauts embarking on a lunar orbit aboard NASA’s Space Launch System (SLS) rocket and Orion spacecraft. Their primary objective is to validate the spacecraft's functionality with crew onboard within the rigorous deep space environment. This pivotal flight test will pave the way for Artemis III, where the first woman will set foot on the Moon. NASA, in conjunction with the Canadian Space Agency (CSA), has disclosed the names of the four astronauts chosen for the Artemis II lunar orbit. The crew comprises Commander Reid Wiseman, Pilot Victor Glover, Mission Specialist 1 Christina Hammock Koch, and Mission Specialist 2 Jeremy Hansen. Together, they will collaborate seamlessly to carry out a series of ambitious demonstrations during the flight test.

THE SUIT

The Orion Suit

Before astronauts launch on Artemis missions to the Moon, they will suit up in a bright orange spacesuit called the Orion Crew Survival System (OCSS). This spacesuit, worn inside NASA’s Orion spacecraft, is designed for a custom fit and equipped with technology features to help protect astronauts on launch day, in emergency situations, throughout high-risk parts of missions near the Moon, and during the high-speed return to Earth.
